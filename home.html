Session One • Prisoner intake begins after sundown

You Wake in Chains.

Cold iron. Wet stone. Gaslight coughing behind blackened glass. Somewhere beyond the bars, a prisoner is praying in a language the city outlawed decades ago.

You do not yet know who arranged your arrest. You know only that Shroudkeep has already decided what happens to people like you.

What Your Character Knows

The city

Shroudkeep, 1891

A sprawling industrial city divided by wealth, smoke, and old blood. The upper districts glitter beneath gaslight while the factory wards choke beneath their chimneys.

The law

Magic Is Forbidden

Arcane practice is treated as sedition. The Sentinels seize charms, books, relics, and anyone accused of using them. Proof is helpful, but not required.

The sickness

The Plague Spreads

Quarantines tighten. Doctors disagree. Entire streets disappear from public records. Wealth appears to be an excellent medicine.

The rulers

Old Families Endure

The oldest houses own factories, banks, hospitals, newspapers, and much of the city itself. Their influence is public. Their true appetites are discussed only in whispers.

The beginning

Blackglass Gaol

Your story begins inside the city's most infamous prison. You may know one another—or you may simply be the only people left alive when something goes terribly wrong.

The warning

Nothing Is Safe

This is a high-stakes gothic campaign. Alliances have costs, violence leaves scars, and survival may require choices that no clean hero would enjoy making.

Before the First Bell

Bring

Your completed level 10 character sheet, a way to take notes, and any questions about features you have never used before.

Decide

What was your character accused of? Whether they actually did it is a separate—and more interesting—question.

Consider

One person in Shroudkeep your character would risk punishment to protect, and one institution they have every reason to despise.

Expect

Investigation, social pressure, dangerous combat, moral compromise, occult horror, and a city that reacts to what you do.
