Lore Before the First Bell

This page contains only knowledge a resident of Shroudkeep could reasonably possess before Session One. Rumor is marked as rumor. Propaganda is left with its teeth showing.

The Founding Families

Public history

The City’s Patrons

Shroudkeep’s oldest families financed its mills, banks, hospitals, rail lines, and civic institutions. Their crests appear on nearly everything worth owning.

Common whisper

They Do Not Age

Portraits painted generations apart bear unsettlingly familiar faces. Servants call it strong blood. The lower wards use less polite language.

Social fact

Blood Opens Doors

Lineage matters in Shroudkeep. Papers, patronage, and family favor can place a person above laws that would crush someone poorer.

Vampires in Shroudkeep

Vampires are not nursery monsters here. They are old wealth, political leverage, inherited terror, and the reason certain doors never open before sunset. Citizens disagree on how many of the ruling class are truly undead. Almost no one doubts that some are.

Invitation & Threshold

Folklore insists a vampire cannot freely enter a private dwelling without invitation. Whether the law recognizes the same boundary is another matter.

Blood & Hunger

Disappearances, private clinics, servant contracts, and “donation registries” have fed decades of rumor. Most citizens know better than to ask who receives the blood.

Sunlight

The oldest families conduct most public business beneath roofs, canopies, fog, and tinted carriage glass. Shroudkeep’s smog is almost civic architecture.

Turning

Tales disagree on how a mortal becomes a vampire. Every version agrees that death is involved—and that returning does not mean returning whole.

The Prohibition of Magic

Magic is forbidden by civic law and prosecuted as a threat to public order. Possession of occult texts, charms, enchanted implements, or unlicensed magical talent may result in seizure, detention, or disappearance. The wealthy are rumored to enjoy private exemptions.

The Plague

The plague moves most fiercely through the Hems and overcrowded parts of Old Town. Quarantine notices appear overnight. Entire households are sometimes removed before dawn. The official papers blame filth, foreign influence, illegal magic, or worker unrest depending on which explanation is most useful that week.

Blackglass Gaol

Built of dark stone and iron-veined glass, Blackglass is where the city holds dissidents, debtors, alleged practitioners, violent criminals, inconvenient witnesses, and anyone whose paperwork has become politically troublesome. Your campaign begins inside it.

Known before play

No one agrees why the prison bells occasionally stop at midnight. No one who works the night corridor will discuss it twice.
