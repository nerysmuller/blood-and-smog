Say What You Attempt

You do not need to know the perfect rule before acting. Describe what your character tries to do. The Dungeon Master will tell you whether it uses an ability check, saving throw, attack roll, or no roll at all.

Your Turn in Combat

Move

Move up to your Speed. You can split movement before and after your action. Standing from prone usually costs half your Speed.

Take One Action

Common actions include Attack, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, Influence, Magic, Ready, Search, Study, and Use an Object.

Bonus Action

You only have something to do here when a spell, feature, or item specifically says it takes a Bonus Action.

Reaction

You usually get one Reaction between the start of your turn and the start of your next turn, but only when a trigger allows it.

Interact

Brief speech and one simple object interaction are usually free: draw a weapon, open an unlocked door, pull a lever, or kick over a chair.

Ask for Help

At level 10, your sheet is a haunted legal document. Pausing to ask what something does is normal and encouraged.

The Rolls You Will Use Most

Ability Check

Roll a d20 and add the relevant ability modifier. Add your Proficiency Bonus when a skill or tool applies and you are proficient.

Saving Throw

Roll when resisting danger: poison, fear, magic, falling debris, or another effect acting upon you.

Attack Roll

Roll a d20, add the listed attack bonus, and compare the total to the target's Armor Class.

Advantage

Roll two d20s and keep the higher. With Disadvantage, keep the lower. They cancel one another and do not stack repeatedly.

Concentration

You may concentrate on only one spell at a time. Taking damage can force a Constitution saving throw to keep it active.

At 0 Hit Points

You fall unconscious and make Death Saving Throws at the start of your turns. Three successes stabilize you; three failures kill you.

Session One Survival

Do not search your sheet for permission to be clever. Barricade doors. Lie. Bargain. Break furniture. Use the environment. Ask what you notice. Help another player. Retreat when a fight becomes a funeral with initiative.
