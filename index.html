The Shroudkeep Chronicle

PLAGUE CURFEW EXTENDED

Citizens of the lower wards are ordered indoors before the ninth bell. Unauthorized gatherings will be dispersed by Sentinel authority.

Factory output remains unaffected. Officials deny reports of missing workers.

Evening Gazette

MAGIC REMAINS A CAPITAL OFFENCE

The Magistracy reminds all citizens that possession of arcane instruments, unlicensed charms, or occult texts constitutes sedition.

Informants may report suspicious conduct anonymously.

The Silk Street Register

FOUNDING FAMILIES HOST WINTER BENEFIT

The city's oldest houses gather beneath gaslight to raise funds for plague relief. Attendance by invitation only.

Servants are advised to present blood papers at the eastern gate.

Morning Dispatch

BLACKGLASS GAOL AT CAPACITY

Officials attribute the increase in arrests to public disorder, vagrancy, and suspected magical contamination.

Trials will proceed without delay.

The Hems Broadside

ANOTHER SHIFT, ANOTHER FUNERAL

Names of the dead have been removed by order of the foreman. Their families are advised that debts remain payable.

Someone is keeping a second list.

Delivered without postmark • Shroudkeep • 1891

Blood & Smog

A summons sealed in red wax.

You have been named, judged, and buried beneath the city's record before ever being heard. At the first bell, Blackglass Gaol will open its cells for inspection.

At midnight, the gaol bells will stop.
